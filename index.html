HOW IT WORKS

Each ant follows simple rules — no central plan, no supervisor.

  • Scouts wander downward, digging through soil. When they've been out long enough they switch to food-seeking mode.
  • Food-seekers follow the cyan pheromone trail you see on the ground — a chemical gradient laid by ants returning from the nest.
  • When a food-seeker finds food, it switches to returning home and lays an amber pheromone trail back to the nest. The stronger the trail, the more ants follow it — this is called stigmergy.
  • Pheromones evaporate over time. If you stop dropping food in one spot, the trail fades and the colony naturally disperses.

Your control: tapping drops food AND immediately floods the area with pheromone signal, so the entire colony pivots toward your target at once. You're not just placing bait — you're rewriting the gradient the whole swarm reads from.
